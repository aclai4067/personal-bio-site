Career Bio

I graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Art Education from Georgia State University in December 2008. After a year as a substitute teacher I moved to Nashville and opened up my job search to look beyond the education field. Since then I've worked in credential evaluation and customer service roles.

In school, I was drawn to art, and especially three dimensional mediums, by the collaborative nature of the field, the desire to build, and the satisfaction of figuring out how to turn an abstract idea into a physical object that works within the limitations of the medium and the laws of physics. In my career, I found my strengths in communication, problem solving, and research.

When I initially broached the idea of a career in software developement, I really wasn't sure it would be for me. While I've always been comfortable with computers and picked up new systems with relative ease, I've never thought of myself as a "computer person." I was surprised to find just how much coding plays to my strenths and the type of tasks that give me satisfaction in my work. It requires the creative problem solving that I found to be my strength both in school and my career. It involves working withing a team and community that fosters improvement through collabortation and critique, as I loved in my Art School days. My research background has helped me in finding solutions when I'm stuck in my code. And it entails a level of continuous learning that is certain to stave off the sense of stagnation that has plagued me in customer services work.
